Step 1: What are Quantum Operators?

Quantum operators are mathematical objects that act on quantum states to produce new quantum states or extract information. They represent physical observables (like position, momentum, energy) and transformations (like rotations, gates). Operators are represented as matrices that act on state vectors: Â|ψ⟩ = |ψ'⟩.

Step 4: Operator Action on States

When an operator acts on a quantum state, it transforms the state according to its matrix representation. For a qubit, operators are 2×2 matrices. The Pauli operators (X, Y, Z) are fundamental: X flips |0⟩↔|1⟩, Z adds a phase to |1⟩, and Y does both. These form the basis for all single-qubit operations.

X|0⟩ = |1⟩, X|1⟩ = |0⟩ (bit flip)
Z|0⟩ = |0⟩, Z|1⟩ = -|1⟩ (phase flip)
