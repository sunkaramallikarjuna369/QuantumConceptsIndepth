Superposition Principle

Interactive exploration of quantum superposition with real-time visualization

Input

Process

Step 1: Quantum State Representation

A qubit in superposition is represented as:
|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩
where α and β are complex numbers called probability amplitudes.

Step 2: Normalization Constraint

For a valid quantum state, the probabilities must sum to 1:
|α|² + |β|² = 1
When auto-normalize is enabled, we adjust the amplitudes to satisfy this constraint.

Step 3: Probability Calculation

The probability of measuring each state is:
P(0) = |α|²
P(1) = |β|²

Step 4: Bloch Sphere Representation

The state is mapped to a point on the Bloch sphere using:
θ = 2·arccos(|α|)
φ = arg(β) - arg(α)
This gives us a 3D visualization of the quantum state.
