Step 1: Wave-Particle Duality Principle

Quantum objects exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties. The behavior observed depends on the experimental setup:

E = hν = ℏω
p = h/λ = ℏk

Where E is energy, p is momentum, λ is wavelength, and h is Planck's constant.

Step 2: Double-Slit Interference

When particles pass through two slits, they create an interference pattern characteristic of waves. The intensity at position x on the screen is:

I(x) = I₀ cos²(πdx/λL)

Where d is slit separation, λ is wavelength, and L is screen distance.

Step 3: Single Particle Detection

Even when particles are sent one at a time, the interference pattern emerges over time. Each particle arrives at a definite location (particle behavior), but the distribution follows a wave pattern.

Step 4: Complementarity Principle

If we measure which slit the particle passes through, the interference pattern disappears. We can observe either wave-like or particle-like behavior, but not both simultaneously.
